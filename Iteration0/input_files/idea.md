**Title: Analytical Deconvolution of Noise-Induced Bias in Energy Decay Dynamics**

This study develops a noise-floor characterization framework by isolating the late-time signal ($t > 15s$) across all 20 oscillators to empirically derive the Gaussian noise variance $\sigma^2$ for the displacement and velocity fields. By applying this variance to the analytical expression for the total energy, we will isolate the non-zero energy bias term—specifically the cross-terms $0.5m(\sigma_v^2)$ and $0.5k(\sigma_x^2)$—that artificially inflates the instantaneous `total_energy` measurement. We will implement a bias-corrected energy trajectory $E_{corrected}(t) = E_{total}(t) - \Delta E_{noise}$ and perform a cycle-averaged integration over the 0–20s window to recover the theoretical dissipation rate. This methodology enables the decoupling of the physical energy decay from the stochastic noise floor, providing a rigorous validation of the energy conservation model in underdamped systems. The resulting framework provides a robust diagnostic for distinguishing true physical damping from measurement-induced energy residuals.
